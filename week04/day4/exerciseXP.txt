#Exercise 1: Introduction to Data Analysis (Easy)

In today’s data driven world, more and more data is being generated every moment. There is even data about data being produced, known as metadata.

Data analysis is a powerful process that enables organizations to collect relevant data, clean it, manipulate it, explore it, and ultimately visualize it in order to gain meaningful insights.
Without the management of clean, accurate, and relevant data, organizations risk losing money, missing opportunities, and operating with critical inaccuracies across teams and objectives.
For example, in a sales organization, if key data points are not tracked such as customer feedback, revenue generated, where it came from, when it was generated, and how it was generated, the organization will struggle to understand what drives success. Without that visibility, it becomes nearly impossible to consistently generate revenue and scale effectively.

Collecting relevant data, scrubbing it, manipulating it, analyzing it, and visualizing it provides tremendous value to any organization.

# Exercise 4: Exploring Data Types

#Identify and list which columns in your dataset are qualitative and which are quantitative.

df_types = df.dtypes

quan_type = []
qual_type = []

for col, dtype in df_types.items():
  if dtype == 'object':
    qual_type.append(col)
  else:
    quan_type.append(col)

print(f"Qualitative types: {qual_type}")
print(f"Quantitative types: {quan_type}")

Qualitative types: ['Species']
Quantitative types: ['Id', 'SepalLengthCm', 'SepalWidthCm', 'PetalLengthCm', 'PetalWidthCm']

The species column is Qualitative because it's simply listing the type of species in string format.
All the other columns are quantitative because they're offering data in either integer or float format.
